Task 3: recode with mutate() and case_when()

Goal: We want to label cars based on their mileage as low MPG (mpg < 15), high MPG (mpg > 25), or medium MPG (otherwise).

mpg_recode <- mtcars %>%
  mutate(mpg_class=case_when(
    mpg > 25 ~ "High MPG",
    mpg < 15 ~ "Low MPG",
    TRUE ~ "Medium MPG"
  ))
table(mpg_recode$mpg_class)

Task 4: recode - name focus

Goal: We want to create a new object called stars_recode which classifies stars according to their temperature (in a new column called temp_group).

Fun fact: I spent about 4 years volunteering / working at the Adler planetarium in Chicago and have done lots of activities with kids about sorting stars according to their temperatures.

# ?dslabs::stars
stars <- dslabs::stars

# View(stars)
colnames(stars)
[1] "star"      "magnitude" "temp"      "type"     
summary(stars$temp)
   Min. 1st Qu.  Median    Mean 3rd Qu.    Max. 
   2500    3168    5050    8752    9900   33600 
stars_recode <- stars %>%
  mutate(
    temp_group = case_when(
    temp < 4000 ~ "Cool",
    temp <= 7500 ~ "Warm",
    TRUE ~ "Hot"
  ))
table(stars_recode$temp_group)

Cool  Hot Warm 
  35   38   23 
head(stars_recode)
            star magnitude temp type temp_group
1            Sun       4.8 5840    G       Warm
2        SiriusA       1.4 9620    A        Hot
3        Canopus      -3.1 7400    F       Warm
4       Arcturus      -0.4 4590    K       Warm
5 AlphaCentauriA       4.3 5840    G       Warm
6           Vega       0.5 9900    A        Hot

Comment: The column temp shows the temperature values but the column temp_group sorts them as cool, warm, or hot (relatively speaking). The new column goes on the left side of the = sign.

Reflection: The order of the case_when() rules matter here because if we’d put the warm rule first then even the cool stars would be classified as warm. Although maybe not! I just wrote the warm rule as <= 7500, but we could have written the warm rule with an and statement (>4000 & <=7500) and then we could have put it first and it would have worked just fine.
