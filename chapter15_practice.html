Task 3 (official): Reflect

In step 6, we used replicate() to test out 1000 different normal distributions of baby lengths to see how often there was a baby over 23”. With rnorm(), we just get a single distribution and can answer the question about one set of babies. But it is helpful to sample lots of sets of babies and replicate() helps us do that. It’s interesting that rnorm() gives one distribution, but it seems like replicate() kind of gives a distribution of distributions!
